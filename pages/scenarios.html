Scenario 1: AI-Generated Content

Your company, which provides data services to online content providers, is working with a new client managing lifestyle websites in cooking, travel, fitness, and health. The client, despite stable ad revenue, is replacing 40% of its image division with AI-generated content. Your team has been tasked with building an interface to integrate AI-generated images, which will be reviewed and approved by the remaining human staff. While the project is moving forward, concerns arise about the ethical and emotional toll of AI-generated content. A team member has abruptly quit, citing the difficulty of moderating such images, leaving you questioning the project's direction and the need to address potential legal, ethical, and human impact issues.

Scenario 2: Using Unauthorized Data for AI Training

In this scenario, you are part of a research lab working on fine-tuning Large Language Models (LLMs) for translating Japanese web novels into English. Initially, the team discovered a high-quality, domain-specific corpus aligned at the sentence level and secured permission from the translators. However, the original authors of the works refuse to grant permission for using their content for AI training. While U.S. and Japanese laws may allow fair use in this context, the moral implications remain uncertain. Team members are divided: some argue for proceeding without consent, others advocate for removing the denied works, and still others suggest abandoning this dataset altogether. Under time pressure and leadership’s insistence, you find yourself questioning whether it’s ethically right to move forward with the entire corpus, knowing it may harm the lab's integrity and trustworthiness in the long run.

Scenario 3: Identity Theft Is Not A Joke

In this scenario, you work at a company that has developed advanced deepfake technology capable of producing highly realistic videos featuring individuals who never gave their approval. A marketing firm wants to use this innovation for celebrity endorsements without seeking the celebrities’ consent. Some colleagues see it as a lucrative opportunity and a chance to stay ahead of the competition. Others worry about the ethical and legal pitfalls, including identity theft, reputational damage, and spreading misinformation. Facing internal pressure to deliver on business objectives, you must decide whether to challenge the decision, risk delaying the project, or comply and potentially contribute to a more deceptive digital world.
